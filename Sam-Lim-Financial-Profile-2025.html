Your position

What's yours, and your share of what's shared

How this is calculated: personal accounts (your salary, EPF, equity unit trust and personal savings) are attributed to you in full. Jointly-held items — both properties, both vehicles, joint cash, PRS, insurance cash values and the children's education fund, along with all liabilities — are split 50/50 between you and Sarah. Your share works out to RM793,000, and Sarah's to RM524,000, summing to the household net worth of RM1,317,000.

Your share is weighted toward retirement savings (EPF) rather than property, since your personal EPF balance is large relative to your half-share of the two properties.

In summary

What's working for you — and where to focus

Where you stand out

  • RM200,000 annual income — the engine that drives nearly 70% of the household's earnings
  • RM350,000 EPF balance, the largest single asset in your personal name
  • Your equity unit trust is up +14.5% on RM55,000 invested — the strongest return of the three family funds

Where to focus

  • Close your RM1,200,000 life-insurance gap — this is the single largest number in this entire report and protects everyone else's plans, not just yours
